Wells+Daily Privacy
Policy & DPA

Effective Date: June 2026

This Global Privacy Policy and Data Processing Addendum ("Privacy Policy") explains how Wells+Daily Inc. ("Brokerage", "we", "us", or "our") collects, processes, shares, and structurally protects the data of our users. This document governs your interaction with our dual-contract enforcement infrastructure, Escrow mechanisms, and proprietary Trust Stack.

Because the Brokerage serves as an actuarial enforcement layer—not a passive marketing platform—our data processing operations are governed by strict contractual locks. By accessing the platform or integrating with our APIs, you expressly consent to the data practices described in this comprehensive policy, which is designed to comply with the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA), the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and the UK GDPR.

1. Scope and Application

1.1. Global Applicability: This Privacy Policy applies to all corporate Brands ("Demand"), individual Creators ("Supply"), site visitors, and authorized third-party integrators globally. Where regional laws mandate specific disclosures (e.g., California, EEA), those specific provisions supersede the general terms only for residents of those jurisdictions.

1.2. Distinguishing Between Controllers and Processors: For the purposes of European data protection laws, Wells+Daily acts as a Data Controller for the account registration, financial routing, and API ingestion processes required to enforce the Bounded Guarantee. We act as a Data Processor when storing isolated, proprietary Brand guidelines or unreleased Creator assets in the Escrow vault prior to validation.

1.3. Acknowledgment of Institutional Use: Our platform is designed strictly for B2B commercial enforcement. We do not provide consumer-facing social media services. Any personal data collected is processed strictly within the context of a commercial enterprise relationship.

2. Exhaustive Data Collection Taxonomy

2.1. Corporate Identity and Verification Data: To comply with federal Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Know Your Customer (KYC) regulations, we collect exhaustive identity data upon onboarding. This includes: full legal names, Doing Business As (DBA) names, corporate structures, Employer Identification Numbers (EIN), Social Security Numbers (SSN for sole proprietors), beneficial ownership data, and government-issued identification scans.

2.2. Deal Framework and Technical Execution Data: We ingest all parameters of the active contract. This includes: gross transaction value (deal sizes), explicit technical briefs, required talking points, brand guidelines, embargo dates, product specifications, and all correspondence routed through our in-platform Escrow and Escalation chat systems.

2.3. Financial, Banking, and Escrow Routing Data: To facilitate the 80/20 payout structure and the 30-day compliance validation hold, we collect bank account details, routing numbers, IBANs, and SWIFT codes. Credit card payments are handled via tokenized, PCI-DSS Level 1 compliant third-party gateways (e.g., Stripe). The Brokerage never stores raw credit card Primary Account Numbers (PANs) on our internal servers.

2.4. Telemetry and Automated Usage Data: Our zero-trust architecture automatically logs metadata to prevent fraud. This includes: IP addresses, geolocations, browser fingerprints, operating systems, session durations, click-path mapping, and authentication timestamps.

3. Authorized Use Cases and Processing Operations

3.1. Executing the Dual-Contract Enforcement Function: The primary legal basis for processing your data is the performance of a contract. We process your technical execution data and API metrics specifically to enforce the 90-day Bounded Guarantee and trigger the release or forfeiture of the 20% compliance hold.

3.2. Curation Underwriting and Risk Profiling: We process historical delivery data, previous escalation rates, and algorithmic fulfillment scores to underwrite our curated roster. Creators with systemic compliance failures are algorithmically culled from the active roster. This processing is based on our legitimate business interest in maintaining the economic survivability of our guarantee.

3.3. Security, Fraud Mitigation, and Threat Intelligence: We process telemetry and identity data to detect unauthorized account access, prevent circumvention of the platform, and protect against distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks.

4. Social API Ingestion and Compliance Holding

4.1. Validated Delivery Verification: The core of our Trust Stack relies on programmatic verification. We ingest public metadata via official APIs (Meta Graph API, YouTube Data API, TikTok API) to verify that an asset has been posted, matches the timeline, and contains the required tracking parameters.

4.2. Monitoring FTC Disclosures: We programmatically parse post descriptions and video metadata during the 30-day validation window to ensure clear and conspicuous FTC disclosures (e.g., #ad) are present and remain unaltered.

4.3. Prohibition on Unauthorized Scraping: The Brokerage relies solely on authorized OAuth permissions and official enterprise APIs. We do not engage in unauthorized, black-hat scraping of social media platforms.

5. Disclosure and Third-Party Processors

5.1. Essential Operational Infrastructure Partners: We strictly limit data sharing to sub-processors required to operate the Brokerage. These include: AWS/GCP for encrypted cloud hosting, Stripe for financial routing, OpenAI/Anthropic for triage escalation support, and specialized KYC/AML verification providers. All sub-processors operate under strict Data Processing Agreements (DPAs).

5.2. Cross-Disclosure Between Matched Counterparties: To facilitate the dual-contract, identifying information (Brand guidelines, Creator profiles) is necessarily shared between the matched Demand and Supply parties executing a specific Deal Framework. This is a fundamental requirement of process ownership visibility.

5.3. Subpoena and Law Enforcement Compliance: We reserve the right to disclose your personal and corporate data without notice if compelled by a court order, subpoena, or lawful request from government authorities, or if necessary to defend against legal claims (e.g., FTC investigations into deceptive advertising).

6. International Data Transfers

6.1. Cross-Border Processing: Wells+Daily is headquartered in the United States. Data collected from individuals or entities residing in the European Economic Area (EEA), the UK, or Switzerland will be transferred to, stored, and processed in the US.

6.2. Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): We ensure an adequate level of protection for international transfers by utilizing the European Commission’s approved Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and adhering to the frameworks established by the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework (DPF) where applicable.

7. Strict Contractual Data Locks vs. Erasure Rights

7.1. Primacy of the Enforcement Function: Because the Brokerage bears the actuarial risk of the 90-day Bounded Guarantee, our data retention policies supersede standard consumer "Right to Delete" requests in specific, active contexts.

7.2. Exemptions from the Right to be Forgotten: You may NOT exercise a right to erasure for any data tied to: (a) an active 90-day guarantee window; (b) an active 30-day compliance validation hold; or (c) an unresolved Escalation Map dispute. The data is contractually locked to preserve evidentiary integrity for arbitration.

7.3. Mandatory Retention for Financial Auditing: Even after the expiration of active contracts, all financial, KYC, and payout routing data is retained in a secure, cold-storage state for a minimum of seven (7) years to comply with IRS and federal tax auditing statutes.

8. Regional Privacy Rights (CCPA, GDPR, UK GDPR)

8.1. Notice at Collection (California Residents): Under the CCPA/CPRA, you have the right to know what personal information we collect, the purpose for its collection, and the categories of third parties with whom it is shared. We do not sell your personal data. We do not share your data for cross-context behavioral advertising.

8.2. Rights of Access, Portability, and Rectification: Subject to the limitations in Section 7, you have the right to request a copy of the data we hold about you in a structured, machine-readable format, and the right to request the correction of inaccurate data.

8.3. Submission of Verifiable Consumer Requests: To exercise your statutory privacy rights, you or your authorized agent must submit a verifiable consumer request to ops@wellsplusdaily.com. We will verify your identity using the documentation provided during onboarding and respond within 45 days (or 30 days under GDPR).

9. Security Infrastructure and Encryption Standards

9.1. AES-256 and TLS 1.3 Protocols: All Escrow vault data, PII, and financial records are encrypted at rest using Advanced Encryption Standard (AES) 256-bit encryption. All data in transit is secured using Transport Layer Security (TLS) 1.3 or higher.

9.2. Zero-Trust Architecture: Internal access to the Escalation Map and production databases is governed by strict zero-trust protocols, role-based access control (RBAC), and mandatory multifactor authentication (MFA) for all Brokerage employees.

10. Cookie Policy and Session Management

10.1. Essential Authentication Tokens: We use strictly necessary cookies and JSON Web Tokens (JWT) to authenticate sessions, secure the Escrow dashboard, and prevent CSRF attacks. You cannot opt-out of these cookies without rendering the platform unusable.

10.2. Analytical Tracking: We use first-party analytical tracking to monitor platform performance and identify Escalation Map bottlenecks. We do not deploy third-party advertising pixels on our authenticated core platform.

11. Modifications and Contact Information

11.1. Material Change Notifications: We reserve the right to update this Privacy Policy to reflect evolving global privacy regulations or changes to our API ingestion models. If we make material changes to how we process your data, we will notify you via the email address associated with your account or through a prominent banner on the platform at least 30 days prior to the changes taking effect.

11.2. Privacy Office Contact Details: If you have questions, concerns, or wish to appeal a data-lock decision, please contact our Data Protection Officer at ops@wellsplusdaily.com.
