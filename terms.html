Agreement

Effective Date: June 2026

This Wells+Daily Services Agreement ("Agreement") is a legal agreement between Wells+Daily Inc. ("Brokerage", "we", "us", or "our") and the corporate entity or person ("you", "your", "Brand", or "Creator") utilizing our enforcement infrastructure, Escrow mechanisms, and associated Trust Stack services (collectively, the "Services").

Please read this Agreement carefully. By accessing, browsing, or otherwise using the Services, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by the General Terms, any applicable Product-Specific Terms (e.g., Escrow and Escalation Terms), and Regional Addendums. If you do not agree to this Agreement, you must not access or use the Services.

1. General Provisions & Architecture

1.1. Scope of the Agreement: This Agreement governs the primary use of the Brokerage. Additional Deal Frameworks, Technical Briefs, or insertion orders generated through our platform are hereby incorporated by reference, but in the event of a conflict between a Deal Framework and this Agreement regarding liability, guarantee fulfillment, or escrow release, this Agreement shall strictly control.

1.2. Role of the Brokerage: Wells+Daily is not an advertising agency, talent management firm, legal representative, or passive matching marketplace. We operate exclusively as an actuarial enforcement layer. We monetize the function of credibly enforcing delivery and compliance at a price point ($5,000–$15,000) where neither Demand nor Supply can credibly enforce on the other.

1.3. Eligibility & Corporate Authority: To use the Services, you must be a legally recognized corporate entity or a sole proprietor operating in a business capacity. If you are entering into this Agreement on behalf of a corporation, limited liability company, or other legal entity, you represent and warrant that you possess the requisite legal authority to bind such entity.

1.4. Independent Contractor Status: Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create a partnership, joint venture, employment, or agency relationship between the Brokerage and any Creator or Brand. Creators act as independent contractors to the Brands they service.

2. The Dual-Contract Framework

2.1. Brand (Demand) Contractual Standing: Brands contract with the Brokerage to access curated Supply and utilize the Bounded Guarantee. Brands do not directly execute employment agreements with Creators via our platform; they execute an enforcement framework through our Escrow systems.

2.2. Creator (Supply) Contractual Standing: Creators contract with the Brokerage to access Deal Frameworks and guarantee their payment through our 80/20 Escrow structure. Creators agree that their compensation is strictly tied to validated delivery metrics monitored by our Trust Stack.

2.3. Exclusivity & Non-Circumvention: During the term of any active Deal Framework and for a period of twelve (12) months thereafter, Brands and Creators introduced via the Brokerage may not circumvent the platform to transact directly. Attempts to bypass the Brokerage’s enforcement layer and escrow fees will result in immediate suspension and liquidated damages equal to 30% of the bypassed transaction value.

3. Execution Risk & Bounded Guarantees

3.1. Conversion of Execution Risk: The core mechanism of the Brokerage is converting unquantifiable execution risk into bounded, priceable operational risk through actuarial curation.

3.2. Scope of the Bounded Guarantee: For Brands, the Brokerage issues a 100% credit-and-replacement guarantee. This guarantee is strictly limited to ensuring that the deliverables map perfectly to the compliance specifications, talking points, and timelines explicitly codified in the Technical Brief.

3.3. Exclusions from the Guarantee: The Bounded Guarantee explicitly excludes: (a) vanity metrics, view counts, engagement rates, or sales conversions; (b) subjective aesthetic disagreements that do not breach the Technical Brief; and (c) any deal where the Brand has failed to meet the required net-payment terms. Unpaid invoices immediately and irrevocably void the Guarantee.

3.4. Timeline Adherence Requirements: The Bounded Guarantee is active for a strict 90-day execution window. Claims for replacement or credit must be filed within this 90-day window via the Escalation Map. Claims filed after Day 90 are automatically rejected.

4. Financial Operations & 80/20 Payouts

4.1. The 80/20 Escrow Tranche Structure: To secure the compliance validation hold, all Creator compensation is disbursed in two tranches. Tranche 1 (80% of the net fee) is released via our integrated payment processors (e.g., Stripe Connect) upon initial validated delivery of the asset.

4.2. The 30-Day Compliance Validation Hold: Tranche 2 (20% of the net fee) is retained by the Brokerage in a non-interest-bearing compliance validation hold. This hold secures the Brand against post-delivery non-compliance, such as unauthorized asset deletion or retroactive removal of FTC disclosures.

4.3. Validation Release: The 20% hold is automatically processed for release on Day 30 post-delivery, provided our API monitoring systems flag no compliance breaches. Release processing times may vary based on banking infrastructure.

4.4. Forfeiture Mechanics: If a Creator breaches the compliance parameters during the 30-day window, the 20% hold is immediately forfeited without right of appeal. The forfeited capital is utilized by the Brokerage to underwrite the Brand's replacement coverage.

4.5. Taxes and Information Reporting: Creators are solely responsible for calculating, reporting, and remitting any applicable taxes to the appropriate tax authorities. The Brokerage or its payment processing partners will issue IRS Form 1099s or equivalent tax documentation as required by law.

5. FTC Compliance & Regulatory Mandates

5.1. Strict Adherence to Endorsement Guidelines: All Supply operating through the Brokerage must strictly comply with the United States Federal Trade Commission (FTC) "Guides Concerning the Use of Endorsements and Testimonials in Advertising" (16 CFR Part 255), as well as equivalent international regulations (e.g., ASA in the UK, AANA in Australia).

5.2. Clear and Conspicuous Disclosures: Creators must include clear, conspicuous, and platform-appropriate disclosures (e.g., #ad, #sponsored) in all promotional content. These disclosures must be visible without the user having to click "more" or expand the description block.

5.3. Liability Transfer: The ultimate legal and financial liability for deceptive advertising rests jointly and severally with the Creator and the Brand. The Brokerage acts as a monitoring layer but explicitly disclaims any liability for regulatory fines, penalties, or lawsuits stemming from improper disclosures or fraudulent claims made by Creators.

6. Intellectual Property (IP) Licenses

6.1. Creator Copyright Retention: Unless explicitly executed via a Full Buyout Addendum in the Deal Framework, Creators retain the underlying copyright to their original content.

6.2. Brand Base Usage Rights: Upon validation of delivery, Brands receive a standard 90-day digital usage license. This license allows the Brand to repurpose, boost, and distribute the delivered assets across owned and operated paid social channels globally.

6.3. Platform Sub-Licensing: Creators grant the Brokerage an irrevocable, royalty-free, worldwide license to ingest, scrape, monitor, and analyze the content via API solely for the purposes of enforcing the compliance validation hold and updating our actuarial models.

6.4. Whitelisting and Advertising Access: Where applicable, Creators must grant the Brand "whitelisting" or advertiser access via native platform tools (e.g., TikTok Spark Ads, Meta Partnership Ads). Revocation of this access during the 90-day execution window constitutes a material breach and grounds for 20% hold forfeiture.

7. Dispute Resolution & Escalation Map

7.1. Mandatory Submission: All disputes regarding delivery, compliance, IP usage, or guarantee fulfillment must be submitted through our proprietary Escalation Map. You agree that external litigation regarding execution disputes is strictly prohibited until the Escalation Map process is exhausted.

7.2. Always-On AI Triage: Initial compliance claims (e.g., missing talking points, timeline breaches) are processed immediately via our AI support infrastructure, which references the locked Technical Brief.

7.3. Human SLA Intervention: Subjective disputes or complex structural remediations are escalated to human operators. Human intervention is strictly bounded by our Service Level Agreement (SLA) operating hours: Monday through Friday, 7:30am to 8:00pm EDT.

7.4. Finality of Determinations: The Brokerage acts as the final arbiter. Our determinations regarding compliance breaches, hold forfeiture, and guarantee fulfillment are final, binding, and not subject to appeal.
